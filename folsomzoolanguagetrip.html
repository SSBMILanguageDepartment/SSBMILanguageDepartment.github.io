Folsom Zoo Language Trip

Welcome to the SSBMI Language Department's Folsom City Zoo Language Trip!

On May 10, 2024, we hosted our second annual outing to the Folsom City Zoo Sanctuary. There, we spoke Nisenan and Miwok with one another while learning about the animals and plants at the zoo. We also hosted several fun activities including a language scavenger hunt and demonstrations by the SSBMI Environmental Department, Exhibits & Collections Center, and Traditional Ecological Knowledge Department.

Here, you can listen to audio recordings of the Nisenan and Northern Sierra Miwok language that we shared in our zoo guidebook and on zoo signage, including animal names, plant names, and more. You can also download a copy of the zoo map that we shared by clicking here.

Animal names

The Folsom City Zoo Sanctuary houses a wide variety of native and non-native animals, many of which have names in both Nisenan and Miwok. Here are the Nisenan and Miwok names for some of the animals who live permanently at the zoo:

English

Barn Owl

Nisenan

Wɨɨt

Miwok

Wíicikiisɨ

Bobcat

Nisenan

Tolomá

Miwok

Tóllomma

Esak’ábe mi? (Do you know?) Many of the animals at the zoo, like horses and parrots, are native to other parts of the world and were only introduced to California within the last few centuries. To name some of these animals, Nisenan and Miwok speakers borrowed their Spanish or English name and adapted it to the sounds of their own language. For example, Nisenan "Kawaayú" and Miwok "Kawáayu" (Horse) both come from the Spanish word "Caballo", which explains why the two words are so similar to one another too!

É’askani? (Did you see it?) While the zoo is not home to any Condors, you may see a metal Condor statue when you visit the zoo. These massive birds once lived throughout California and were known to Nisenan and Miwok speakers too. Here are their Nisenan and Miwok names:
