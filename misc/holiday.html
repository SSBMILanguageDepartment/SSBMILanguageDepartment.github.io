SSBMI Holiday Language 2025

Wenném Tamasím K’aw! (Happy Wintertime!) The SSBMI Language Department wishes you all a great holiday season and a Happy New Year.

Here, you can listen to the Nisenan and Northern Sierra Miwok language that we shared on the Tribe's holiday card, at the holiday party, and elsewhere this year. You can also find additional resources for learning more language and learn more about the Tribe's heritage languages.

Nisenan holiday language

Séekas’in!

Hello!

Wenném Tamasím K’aw!

Happy Wintertime!

Honk’ayheesé wadáawesi meem hedém k’awí.

We hope you all will be well this year.

Northern Sierra Miwok holiday language

Micɨ́ksɨs!

Hello! ("How are you?")

Kucí’ Umúuca’!

Happy Wintertime!

Kucíiyissɨ’ kucí’’aciŋkɨnni’mok ney umúucay.

We hope you all will be well this year.

About the Nisenan and Northern Sierra Miwok languages

The SSBMI Community has ancestral ties to the Nisenan language through the Matriarchs, Pamela Cleanso Adams and Annie Hill Murray Paris.

Nisenan has been spoken throughout what is now Sacramento, Amador, El Dorado, Sutter, and Placer Counties since time immemorial. Pamela and many of her close relations spoke the Valley dialect of Nisenan and passed on most of the knowledge that we have today about this dialect. Based on where she was from and the language of her relations, Annie likely spoke the Southern Hill dialect of Nisenan. Additionally, the Tribe has ancestral ties to Nisenan villages like Pusúune, K’ademmá, Wóllok, and Yáales, where Nisenan has been spoken since time immemorial.

We hope you will join us in celebrating the Tribe's heritage by embracing the Nisenan and Northern Sierra Miwok languages this year!
