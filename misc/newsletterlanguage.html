Nisenan Language in the Tribal Member Newsletter

Starting in May 2025, we are including more Nisenan language in the Tribal Member Newsletter including as part of the newsletter's new title and throughout the sections of the newsletter. Here, you can listen to the language in the newsletter and learn more.

On the cover

The newsletter received a new title recently - Homonáan ɨdáwkani? This is a traditional Nisenan greeting which the Tribe's Ancestors shared, which literally means 'Where are you coming from?' However, you'll often see it translated more loosely as 'Where have you been?'

The Tribal Member newsletter often focuses on where Tribal Members and staff have been, and so we feel that this is an appropriate title.

English

Where are you coming from?

(said to 1 person)

Nisenan

Homonáan ɨdáwkani?

Esak’ábe mi? (Do you know?) You can learn more traditional Nisenan greetings like this one on our greetings webpage.

You will also find the name of the current season on the cover. You may recognize these names from seasonal dances like Yoomením Payyohó (Spring Dance) and materials we've shared like seasonal coloring pages and language in the Environmental Department's 2025 calendar.

English

Winter

Nisenan

Tamás

Spring

Yoomén

Summer

Okomén

Fall

Topmén

Inside the newsletter

Inside the newsletter, you will find the message from Chairwoman titled Mayením Pedawím, which means 'Chairwoman Speaks' or 'Chairwoman Makes a Speech'. The word mayén means 'chairwoman, headwoman' and is the name for a traditional community headwoman.

English

Chairwoman Speaks

Nisenan

Mayením Pedawím

Chairwoman, Headwoman

Mayén

You will find the words Wenném Hoyyahám Okó (Happy Birthday) in the section announcing Tribal Member birthdays. There is not a traditional equivalent for 'Happy Birthday'. Rather, this phrase literally means 'Good First Day' and is what we've shared for 'Happy Birthday' before.

English

Happy Birthday!

Nisenan

Wenném Hoyyahám Okó!

About the Nisenan language

The SSBMI Community has ancestral ties to the Valley and Southern Hill dialects of the Nisenan language through the Tribe's Matriarchs, Pamela Cleanso Adams and Annie Hill Murray Paris. Pamela spoke the Valley dialect and she, her brother Tom Cleanso, and her daughter Lillie Williams are responsible for passing on most of the knowledge we have of Valley Nisenan today. We have less direct information about Annie’s language; based on where she was from and documented language from her relations and associates, she likely spoke Southern Hill Nisenan.

The language that we share in the Tribal Member Newsletter comes from speakers of both of the SSBMI Community's heritage dialects, Valley Nisenan and Southern Hill Nisenan. Where we coined new phrases, such as Wenném Hoyyahám Okó for 'Happy Birthday', we combined Nisenan words using Nisenan word-formation rules that these same speakers use when speaking their language.

Esak’ahá daak’ábe mi c’aykɨ́ɨ? (Do you want to know more?)

For more help reading these words and phrases, please consult our Guide to the SSBMI Nisenan Alphabet.

If you have any questions about the language or about receiving a copy of the cards, please contact us at language@ssband.org.
