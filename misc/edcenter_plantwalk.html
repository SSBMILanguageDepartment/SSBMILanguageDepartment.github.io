Ed Center Plant Walk

This is a temporary webpage for sharing audio recordings of Nisenan plant names for the Ed Center plant walk. Please do not share widely.

Bətím Wené

Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)

C’ak

Yerba Santa (Eriodictyon californicum)

C’ítok’

Poison Oak (Toxicodendron diversilobum)

Hoo

Soaproot (Chlorogalum pomeridianum)

K’ak’

Black Elderberry tree (Sambucus nigra)

Heads-up: Remember that there's a different word for the elderberry tree, k’ak’, versus the berries of the elderberry tree, áantay.

Áantay

Black Elderberry berry (Sambucus nigra)

K’otó

Common Manzanita (Arctostaphylos manzanita)

Lɨl

Western Redbud (Cercis occidentalis)

Munmún

Mugwort (Artemisia douglasiana)

Heads-up: Remember that mugwort (Artemisia douglasiana) and white sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) are both called: munmún.

Munmún

White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana)

Heads-up: Remember that mugwort (Artemisia douglasiana) and white sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) are both called: munmún.

Musk’úlk’ul

Rosehip, Wild rose (Rosa californica)

Pallá

Valley Oak (Quercus lobata)

Piitó

Deergrass (Muhlenbergia rigens)

Pikí

Blue Oak (Quercus douglasii)

Poló

California Buckeye (Aesculus californica)

Heads-up: You can learn more about how buckeye balls are used as a traditional food source on our buckeye ball processing page.

Toon

Foothill Gray Pine (Pinus sabiniana)

Yoolós

Toyon (Heteromeles arbutifolia)

Esak’ahá daak’ábe mi c’aykɨ́ɨ? (Do you want to know more?)

The Language Department created this webpage in order to share audio recordings of Nisenan plant names with Ed Center and Environmental Department staff as we prepare to host a plant walk for the Ed Center Youth. The photographs on this page come from Wikimedia Commons and Calscape and have different permissions associated with them; this page is for internal use only.
